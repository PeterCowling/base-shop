Hostel Brikette Travel Guide

Tip

Need other transport options? Open the How to Get Here overview for ferries, taxis, and mixed itineraries.

Journey overview

  • This route is two SITA bus legs: first from Positano (Chiesa Nuova) to Amalfi, then from Amalfi to Salerno.
  • Allow extra time in high season for queues and traffic.

SITA buses and tickets

  • SITA Sud operates the coastal buses between Positano, Amalfi, and Salerno.
  • Buy tickets before boarding at a tabacchi/newsstand or cafe (payment options vary).
  • Keep your ticket ready; the driver typically stamps or tear-validates it when you board.

Timetable and planning

Positano (Chiesa Nuova) bus stop

/img/directions/positano-bus-stop.png

Positano's Chiesa Nuova SITA stop outside Bar Internazionale

Arrive a few minutes early to secure a seat; buses can fill quickly in summer.

  • The closest SITA stop is Positano (Chiesa Nuova) on Via G. Marconi.
  • It sits about 140 metres (490 feet) from the hostel entrance.
  • You will wait outside the cafe/bar called 'Bar Internazionale'.

Walk from the hostel to the stop

  • Exit the hostel, walk down the steps to the main road, and turn left.
  • Continue along Via G. Marconi for roughly 100 metres (330 feet).
  • Bar Internazionale will be on your left; the SITA stop is right outside.

Buy your ticket and wait for boarding

  • Buy a SITA bus ticket to Amalfi from the bar or nearby newsstand.
  • Cross to the far side of the road and queue for the Amalfi-bound bus.
  • Double-check the bus display – it should read 'Amalfi'.

Change buses at Amalfi

  • Alight at Amalfi’s main bus terminal beside the waterfront piazza.
  • Locate the stand for Salerno-bound SITA buses; ask staff if unsure.
  • If needed, buy a fresh ticket for the Amalfi → Salerno leg before boarding.

Arriving in Salerno

  • SITA buses for Salerno terminate near the city transport hubs. From there you can continue by local bus, walk to Piazza della Concordia and the ferry port, or reach Salerno train station. Always follow on-site signage or ask staff for the most direct connection.
