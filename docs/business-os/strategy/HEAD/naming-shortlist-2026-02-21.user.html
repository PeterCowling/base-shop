Research context

Naming constraints & strategic territory

Recommended territory

Coined word with Italian-accessible phonetics, lifestyle-forward positioning. The name must avoid clinical register (no "hearing", "ear", or device-outcome language), feel warm and parent-safe, and stretch naturally beyond the launch headband SKU into a full accessories range (pouches, patches, clips).

Hard constraints

  • .com freely available at standard registration cost ‚Äî parked or brokered domains treated as hard blockers
  • No EU MDR framing risk in the name itself ‚Äî avoid roots that imply the product enables or improves cochlear implant function
  • No clinical register ‚Äî avoid "hearing", "ear", "implant", "sound", "therapy" roots
  • No prior business/consumer brand collision ‚Äî coined words substantially reduce this risk

MDR framing note (Pattern B names)

Carryina (rank 3, from "carry") and Anchoreno (rank 19, from "anchor") require careful copy review. The names themselves are coined and non-descriptive, but copy using these roots to imply securing/holding the cochlear processor in place risks EU MDR accessory-enabling framing. Use functional lifestyle copy ("for active days", "made for everyday wear") rather than device-outcome copy.

Pattern guide ‚Äî what each generation method means

Pattern A ‚Äî Morpheme combination

Two invented Italian-phonetic morpheme stems combined into a single word. Produces longer names (7-10 letters) with natural internal rhythm and strong TM ownability. Dominant pattern in this pool (68 of 150 available names).

Pattern B ‚Äî English-root respelling

A functional English root (carry, anchor, comfort) respelled with an Italian suffix, removing plainly English character while retaining a subliminal functional cue. Requires copy review for EU MDR framing where the root implies device function.

Pattern C ‚Äî Fragment blend

Two phonetic fragments joined at a natural junction. Produces shorter, flowing names (6-8 letters). Second-largest pattern group (32 of 150 available).

Pattern D ‚Äî Root + suffix (spoken name ‚â† domain)

Short invented root combined with a descriptive suffix to form the domain string, while the spoken brand name is the root alone. Enables a very short spoken name (Semira, Tireva) with a distinctive domain string.

Pattern E ‚Äî Real Italian word, non-descriptive in class

An obscure or archaic Italian word with no descriptive meaning in EUIPO Classes 10 or 25. Tramaio (a type of net; non-descriptive for headbands) and Tarlatana (a loosely woven cloth; borderline ‚Äî verify with counsel) are the shortlist representatives.
