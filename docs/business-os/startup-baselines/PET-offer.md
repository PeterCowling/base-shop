# PET Dog Accessories — Offer Design

**Executive summary:** A premium dog lifestyle accessories brand targeting urban millennial female
dog owners who treat their dog as a lifestyle extension. Product 1 is a mini Birkin-style poop
bag holder with leash clip at €80 — positioned between Atlas Pet Co ($45 craft) and LISH London
(£65 leather), well below Pagerie ($152). What makes this different: genuinely usable craft
quality, a no-fault exchange policy (Pagerie's single biggest failure point), and a design that
makes the walk *feel* put together. Brand scope is intentionally broad — full dog lifestyle
accessories line — with the holder as the demand test product.

---

## Section 1: ICP Segmentation

### ICP-A: The Dog Mum (Primary — 80% of launch buyers)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 25–40
- Location: urban centres (Milan, London, Barcelona, Amsterdam, Paris) — cities with café culture
  and dog-welcome spaces; also coastal/resort towns where dogs are carried or walked in
  fashionable contexts
- Income: €35,000–€80,000 household (comfortably spending €80 without a second thought on
  something she *loves*, even if she'd pause at €150)
- Device: mobile-first; discovers via Instagram Reels, TikTok, Etsy search
- She doesn't have children yet — or has made a conscious choice. Her dog is *her dog*, not
  the family pet.

**Psychographics:**
- Values: aesthetics, quality over quantity, brand story, sustainability signals, Italian/
  European craft. Not a logo-chaser — more "quiet luxury" than status display.
- Fear: looking like she "tried too hard" — she wants to look effortlessly considered, not
  ostentatious. She won't buy the £300 Pagerie harness because it screams money. She'll buy
  the €80 Italian leather holder because it looks like *taste*.
- Aspiration: to be the version of herself on a walk that matches how she feels in the rest
  of her life — curated, intentional, well-dressed. Her dog is an extension of that.
- Buying style: researches before buying (Instagram → website → Etsy → reviews), makes
  emotionally-driven decisions she then rationalises logically. Uses the word "splurge" to
  grant herself permission.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Functional: carry poop bags on a walk without it looking utilitarian or ugly
- Emotional: feel *put together* — "I'm the dog mum who has it together"
- Social: earn compliments from strangers and community recognition in the dog-owner world

**Buying triggers:**
- Sees a beautiful holder on someone else's dog at the park and asks "where did you get that?"
- Gets a new dog / first dog — the "setting up" purchase moment
- Replaces a broken or ugly plastic dispenser that's been annoying her for months
- Gifting occasion: dog's "birthday", new dog gift, Christmas self-treat

### ICP-B: The Gift-Giver (Secondary — ~20% of launch buyers)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28–55 (broader — can be a parent, sibling, friend of the primary ICP)

- Budget allocated: €70–€100 (gift budget — slightly less price-sensitive than a self-purchase
  because "it's a gift")

**Psychographics:**
- Buying for: someone who fits ICP-A exactly
- Fear: getting something generic or cheap-looking when the recipient has "taste"
- Aspiration: to be the friend who got the perfect gift — not another candle or wine

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Functional: find a high-quality, distinctive gift for a dog-owner friend

- Social: strengthen the relationship through a demonstrably considered gift

**Buying triggers:**
- Dog birthday (44% of dog owners buy birthday gifts for their pets)
- New dog gift ("welcome to dog ownership")
- Christmas / birthday — "she already has everything but doesn't have this"
- Recent break-up or life event where the dog is particularly important to the recipient

## Section 3: Offer Structure

### Core Offer

**Product:** Mini Birkin-style dog poop bag holder with brass leash clip
- Materials: leather or high-quality leather-look body (same as HBAG line), brass-finish leash clip
  hardware, magnetic clasp closure
- Includes: 1 roll of poop bags (enough for the walk that tests the holder)
- Dimensions: holds 1 standard roll of poop bags; designed for one-hand access
- Attachment: clips to any standard dog lead, belt loop, or bag strap
- Available: single colourway at launch (to be confirmed from /lp-offer brand palette work)

**Price:** €80 (confirmed by Pete 2026-02-17)

**Delivery:** Italy-origin; EU + international shipping. Timeframe TBD from supplier.

### Bundles / Tier Variants

At launch: single SKU (poop bag holder). No bundles pre-checkpoint.

Post-checkpoint candidate bundles (not in scope for demand test):
- Dog Walk Set: holder + matching lead charm
- Gift Set: holder + drawstring gift pouch + handwritten note card

### Included Deliverables

- Holder itself (with 1 roll of bags)
- Tissue + ribbon packaging (positioned as giftable out of the box)
- Handwritten note from maker (the Etsy emotional differentiator — replicable at small scale)
- Care card (leather/faux-leather care instructions)

### Exclusions

- No dog lead (holder clips to existing lead; leads are not sold at launch)
- No personalisation at launch (name engraving, breed charm — deferred to post-10 sales)
- No subscription / auto-refill bags service

### Guarantees

- **30-day free exchange** on any order (no fault required — wrong colour, doesn't feel right,
  changed mind). One exchange per order. This is the direct counter to Pagerie's fatal flaw.
- **Quality guarantee:** if the hardware fails within 90 days of normal use, replacement at no cost.

### Risk Reversals

- Generous exchange policy removes the biggest premium purchase blocker
- No sizing anxiety (leash clip design eliminates fit variables that plague harness/collar purchases)
- "Try it on your walk" implicit: the stakes of a failed purchase are low (clip it on, use it once,
  exchange if it's not right)

## Section 4: Positioning One-Pager

### Positioning Statement (Moore Template)

```
For urban millennial female dog owners, 25–40,
who treat their dog as a lifestyle extension but refuse to carry something ugly on their walks,
[BRAND NAME] is a premium dog lifestyle accessories brand
that turns the most unglamorous moment of dog ownership into something that looks considered —
unlike Pagerie (too expensive, terrible returns) or Lucy & Co (too casual, wrong aesthetic),
because we craft our accessories from the same leather body as a mini Birkin,
at €80, with a 30-day no-fault exchange.
```

### Category

Existing category: premium/artisan dog accessories. We are not creating a new category — we are
occupying the white space *between* accessible-fashion (Lucy & Co at $14) and ultra-luxury
(Pagerie at $152). The gap is real, documented, and currently occupied only by LISH London
(£65, operational failures) and Atlas Pet Co ($45, function-not-fashion focus).

### Competitive Frame

Customers compare us to:
1. Other Etsy artisan sellers ($25–60 range) — our primary frame at launch
2. LISH London (£65) — the direct quality analogue; our exchange policy is better
3. Pagerie (for aspirational buyers who baulked at $152) — we are "Pagerie at a real-world price"

### Key Message

*The walk, put together.*

(Secondary message: "At €80, it costs less than a night out. And it will outlast both.")

## Section 5: Pricing / Packaging Hypothesis

**THIS IS A HYPOTHESIS TO VALIDATE. €80 is the starting price for the demand test.**

### Proposed Price Point

| Variant | Price | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Dog poop bag holder (leash clip) | **€80** | Positions between Atlas ($45 craft) and LISH (£65 leather); below Pagerie ($152); anchors the brand as "premium without the price shock" |

### Pricing Model

One-time purchase. No subscription. No tiers at launch.

### Competitor Price Comparison

| Brand | Price | Positioning | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Earth Rated / generic dispensers | €5–€10 | Commodity | Plastic, no fashion signal; the product this replaces |
| Lucy & Co | $9.80–$14 | Mid-fashion | Neoprene, pattern-led; "affordable lifestyle" — different ICP |
| Sassy Woof | $12.99–$15.99 | Licensed IP (Barbie, Disney) | Different buyer — licensed fan, not lifestyle premium |
| Atlas Pet Company | $35–$45 | Craft premium | Recycled materials, lifetime warranty; function-first; no fashion signal |
| LISH London | £49–£65 | Luxury leather | Chrome-free vegetable-tanned; strongest direct competitor; operational failures create opening |
| **THIS BRAND** | **€80** | **Premium craft, fashion-forward** | **The white space: between Atlas ($45) and LISH (£65)** |
| Pagerie "The Pochette" | $152 | Ultra-luxury | Full leather pochette; top of market; ICP overlaps but price excludes most buyers |

### Anchor Pricing

Pagerie at $152 is the aspirational ceiling. Our €80 is "everything Pagerie buyers wanted — leather
aesthetic, Italian craft, the look — at a price that doesn't require a return drama to justify."

### Price Positioning

Premium. Not accessible-budget (€15–30), not ultra-luxury (€150+). The premium craft tier (€60–120)
is the real white space.

### Rationale

1. **Competitive gap:** No brand owns €60–100 premium fashion-forward dog accessories in the EU market.
   LISH is the closest analogue at £65 but has operational failures and limited distribution.
2. **Willingness-to-pay evidence:** Pagerie reviews show buyers comfortable at $152 for the concept;
   the only complaints are about execution (returns, sizing), not sticker price.
3. **Same-body supply chain:** Using the same mini bag body as HBAG (cost structure established)
   makes €80 viable at expected contribution margin (≥35% target, subject to TASK-03 supplier cost).
4. **Etsy artisan premium ceiling:** Premium leather Etsy sellers top out at $50–60. €80 requires
   brand context beyond Etsy — which the Instagram/TikTok probe tests directly.

### Confidence Level

**MEDIUM.** Evidence supports €80 as viable for a buyer who *understands the brand*. The risk:
Etsy buyers may not have sufficient context from a listing alone to justify €80 without seeing
the brand story. Instagram/TikTok probe tests whether brand context drives price acceptance
beyond the Etsy listing frame.

**What would make this HIGH confidence:** 3 paid sales at €80 with no price objections in DMs.

### Validation Plan

1. TASK-06/07: Open Etsy listing at €80; run Instagram probe for 28 days.
2. Track: Do DM inquirers ask "is this the price?" (price shock signal) or move straight to "how
   do I buy?" (acceptance signal).
3. Checkpoint at 28 days: if 0 sales and DM inquiries include price objections, test €65 for
   2 weeks before concluding demand is absent.
4. If 3+ sales at €80 with no price complaints: confidence moves to HIGH; pricing confirmed.
