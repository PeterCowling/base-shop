# Market Intelligence Pack: HEAD - Cochlear Implant Headbands in Italy

## A) Executive Summary

- Italy cochlear implant installed base is meaningful but niche: about 17,000 users.
- Italian procedure flow is material and appears to have grown over time.
- Headband retention products are clearly active in Italian specialist retail.
- Observed visible price corridor is broad (roughly low-teens EUR to premium ~EUR65).
- Etsy and Amazon surfaces show established marketplace behavior for this category.
- Fastest route to first sales is likely marketplace-first plus lightweight DTC.
- Segment sequencing should start with highest urgency users (caregivers/children), then sport-active users, then daily-comfort adults.
- Economics can work but are highly sensitive to conversion, CAC, and especially returns.
- Checkout/payments must align with Italian buyer expectations (cards/wallets, with PayPal strong).
- Regulatory boundary risk is material: accessory-to-medical-device interpretation under MDR can change obligations.
- GPSR applies, so safety/traceability requirements are live.
- Core recommendation: validation-led launch, disciplined claims, and aggressive early measurement.

## B) Business Context and Assumptions

- Business intent: B2C brand for cochlear implant users, starting with retention headbands.
- Launch surface: `pre-website`.
- Stock posture: initial stock already purchased; assume near-term sellable availability (<=14 days) until exact date is confirmed.
- Budget posture: avoid large blind paid spend; set from TAM constraints and early measured economics.
- Operating posture: 95/5 split toward direct sales execution vs CMS migration.

## G) Unit Economics Priors (Validation Stage)

Directional priors for early planning:
- Single-unit price corridor: roughly EUR18-EUR32 for core tier.
- AOV potential: improved by bundle mechanics.
- CVR prior: low-single-digit range pending measured data.
- Returns prior: material risk driver and must be actively controlled.
- CAC prior: highly uncertain pre-launch; must be measured in week 1-2.
- Margin prior: feasible only if fit/returns/support friction are managed.

## H) Channel Strategy (First 90 Days)

Recommended sequence:
1. Marketplace-first demand capture (Etsy first; Amazon if operationally feasible).
2. Minimal DTC surface for trust/FAQ/compatibility and assisted checkout.
3. Community/association-led trust channels for low-CAC signal.
4. Paid expansion only after evidence gates pass.

## I) Website Design Implications

Minimum website requirements for launch:
- Use-case-led IA (Kids / Sport / Daily).
- Compatibility selector and sizing guidance.
- PDP clarity on fit, mic exposure considerations, and care instructions.
- Clear returns/withdrawal and support pathways.
- Italy-suitable payment options (cards + PayPal minimum).
- Privacy/cookie compliance from day one.

## J) Product Design Implications

Must-have design constraints:
- Secure retention without microphone obstruction.
- Unilateral and bilateral wearing support.
- Child-safe retention approach where relevant.
- Explicit sizing model tied to head circumference.
- Clear compatibility mapping by processor family.

Failure modes to test early:
- Slippage under sweat/motion.
- Fit discomfort/pressure.
- Mic interference due to placement.
- Fastener durability/usability issues.

## K) Regulatory and Claims Constraints

Key constraints:
- MDR accessory boundary risk must be handled carefully (claims + intended use language matter).
- GPSR obligations apply.
- Textile labeling obligations apply.
- Distance-selling withdrawal rights and legal guarantee obligations must be reflected in policy and operations.
- Child-safety hazards (for cords/attachments) must be proactively designed out or mitigated.

Red lines:
1. No medical efficacy claims.
2. No unsafe child-oriented attachment design.
3. No DTC launch without compliant withdrawal/returns/legal identity setup.
