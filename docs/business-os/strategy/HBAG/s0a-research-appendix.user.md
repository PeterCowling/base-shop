### F4. Retention Model: One-Off vs. Recurring

| Metric | Benchmark | Source |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Fashion/apparel repeat purchase rate (industry) | 20–25% within 180 days | Mobiloud 2026 |
| FOMO-driven purchase within 24h (scarcity drops) | 60% of buyers cite FOMO | Queue Fair |
| Artisan goods with 4+ week lead times | Very few repurchases within 3 months; repurchase starts at 6 months | Peel Insights |

**Structural reality:** Artisan bag accessories at €80–€150 are predominantly **one-off or low-frequency** in the base case. The item isn't consumed or worn out quickly. Repurchase drivers:

1. **Collecting behaviour** — seasonal colourways and capsule drops bring buyers back for new pieces (tan → blue → limited edition). Collector segment (top 10–20%) will buy 3–4× per year.
2. **Gift giving** — a self-buyer returns for birthdays, holidays, Valentine's/Mother's Day. Maps to Q4 and gift-occasion calendar.
3. **Drop culture mechanics** — limited edition seasonal drops with scarcity messaging. 60% of FOMO purchases happen within 24 hours. Highest-leverage retention tool available to a small artisan brand at launch.

**Year 1 target:** 15–20% repeat rate. Year 2+ with drop culture and email list: 25–35%.

### G2. Fast Trust Signals: Converting the 3-Minute Tourist Decision

**Ranked by leverage for HBAG:**

1. **Provenance card (highest leverage)** — Physical card: where made (Positano/Italy), by whom (maker name), from what (leather type), brief process note ("hand-stitched vegetable-tanned leather"), QR to Instagram/product page. Transforms anonymous object into traceable item. Positano origin is itself a luxury provenance signal.

2. **Display quality and environment** — Premium risers, uncluttered cases, ambient lighting. Proximity to established brands in a multi-brand boutique provides halo effect. Never stack items; never display on a flat table with no elevation.

3. **Price visibility + credential** — Price must be immediately visible and framed. At €80–€150, it reads correctly only alongside a brief credential: "Made by hand in Positano, Italy." Unmarked or hidden price = friction that signals the maker is ashamed of it.

4. **Tactile access** — Display product touchably. Behind-glass at €80–€150 introduces a barrier that costs sales. "Please handle" signal if needed.

5. **Social proof** — "@HBAG — 4.9 stars, 200 customers" with QR to Instagram. At zero reviews, substitute: a photo of the maker working is an authenticity signal. Social proof can be built from day one.

6. **Packaging that travels well** — Compact, beautiful box or branded pouch communicates gift-readiness and solves the tourist's "how do I get this home safely" problem. Etsy data: 72% of customers say custom packaging influences purchases.

*Source: synthesised from artisan retail practitioner frameworks, Etsy packaging data, Crazy Egg trust signal research, BigCommerce ecommerce trust signals. No Positano-specific study found.*

### G4. Operational Risks: Seasonal Inventory

| Risk | Quantification | Source |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Post-season returns | 15–30% of seasonal volume | Uphance |
| Cash flow from dead stock | Immediate margin erosion; capital locked | NUL Global |
| Trend cycle shortened | One forecasting miss = excess stock requiring markdowns | Easy Replenish |

**HBAG-specific risks:**
1. **Summer peak concentration:** June–September is nearly all the Amalfi season. Small batches of 5–10 units per SKU — replenish only what sells.
2. **Consignment dead stock risk:** 90-day consignment window maximum; monthly payout cycle means capital locked up to 120 days. 20 items × €40 COGS = ~€800 of working capital per boutique partner.

4. **Cash flow cycle:** Consignment = not paid until item sells. Budget for this lag in working capital planning.

## I. Proof Tests (ASSESSMENT-01 → ASSESSMENT-02 Gate)

### I1. Seven-to-Fourteen Day Proof Test Designs

**a) Social media demand probe (minimum investment)**

1. Create 3–5 Reels (7–15 sec each): texture/ASMR close-up, "pack with me" context, provenance story. Post across 7 days.
2. Link-in-bio to minimal landing page (Linktree / Carrd) with single CTA: "Join waitlist / DM to buy." Track link clicks and DM volume.
3. Optional: boost best-performing reel with $50–$100 Instagram paid promotion targeting women 28–50 interested in travel, Italy, fashion accessories, leather goods.
4. Measure on day 7 and 14: follower growth, saves per reel, link-in-bio clicks, DMs received, waitlist sign-ups.

**b) Etsy demand test (valid, not just listing)**

A new Etsy shop has zero organic search visibility. A valid test requires driving external traffic:

1. Create 1 Etsy listing with 7–10 professional photos, keyword-optimised title/tags. **Price at intended retail — do not discount; discounting invalidates the price test.**
2. Drive 200–500 visitors via: Instagram posts linking to Etsy, Reddit communities (r/handbags, r/femalefashionadvice), $20–$30 Etsy promoted listings.
3. Measure over 14 days: views, favourites, buyer messages, purchases.

Interpretation:
- Views with <1% favourites → photography or presentation problem, not product rejection
- 5%+ favourites, 0 purchases → price sensitivity or trust friction (no reviews) — test a "first buyer 10% off" DM
- 1–2 purchases from 200–500 driven visitors → purchase intent confirmed at price point

**c) In-person / pop-up proof test**

1. Request 1-week consignment trial with an existing Positano boutique, OR obtain a "licenza di vendita temporanea" (Italian temporary street market licence) for an artisan fair.
2. Display: 10–15 items maximum, clean linen-covered table, 3–5 pieces at eye level, provenance card beside each, Instagram handle on small sign. No more than 8–10 SKUs (choice overload reduces CVR).
3. Duration: 2–3 market days or 7–14 day consignment window during peak season (July–August) or shoulder seasons (May–June).
4. Staffing: maker present for some sessions — provenance authenticity is most powerful when personalised.

**Conversion targets:**

| Metric | Minimum pass | Strong pass |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Browser-to-buyer (of people who handle) | 5–8% | 15%+ |
| Footfall-to-purchase | 1–3% | 5%+ |
| Avg transaction value | At or above €80 target | |
| Transactions in 2–3 days (peak season) | 3–5 | 10+ |

### I3. Fastest Falsification Signal

**The thesis is falsified when:**

> High expressed interest (saves, favourites, DMs, product handling in-person) + zero purchases at €80–€150 across all three channels simultaneously.

This separates product-market fit (do people want this?) from price-willingness (will they pay €100 for it?).

| Signal | Meaning | Does NOT mean |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| 500 Etsy views, 30 favourites, 0 purchases | Price/trust gap at €100+ | Product rejection |
| 10,000 impressions, 200 saves, 0 DM inquiries | Content-to-commerce disconnect or wrong audience reached | Product rejection |
| 5 DMs asking price, 0 converting when told €100 | Price is the barrier; product desire exists | Product rejection |
| 20 in-person handles, 0 purchases, 3 say "lovely but a bit much" | Price needs testing; consider €55–€70 entry SKU | Product rejection |

**The thesis is NOT falsified by:**
- Low social engagement with no paid amplification (channel problem)
- Zero Etsy sales with zero driven traffic (visibility problem)
- Low in-person sales at an off-season or low-footfall event

**Fastest single test:** Drive 500 targeted visitors to one Etsy listing at full price via $50 Instagram promoted post + $20 Etsy promoted listings over 10 days.
- 500 views → 0 purchases, <10 favourites: thesis under serious challenge
- 500 views → 5–20 favourites, 0 purchases: price/trust issue — test price adjustment or review-building
- 500 views → 2+ purchases: purchase intent confirmed, move to scale economics

Sources: CXL smoke test guide | Founder FAQs | Amra & Elma | eXimius VC | Horizon

## A. Segment Truth

### A1. Minimum Handbag Tier Where "Reads Premium in Hand" Matters

**Finding: Approximately the LV/Gucci entry tier (~€600–€1,200 bags), not Coach/MK.**

- **Coach / Michael Kors tier (€300–€800 bags):** These buyers purchase charms, but their frame is "cute and affordable personalisation," not "craft that reads premium." Coach CEO Todd Kahn described the charm strategy as building "an ecosystem where customers can continually refresh and reimagine what they already own" — the language of frequency, not prestige. The Coach $95 charm is priced to be grabbed at checkout without deliberation. Source: Glossy / Coach "Bag First, Charm Later."

- **LV / Gucci / Prada tier (€800–€2,500 bags):** The charm must "hold its own" against the bag. Bloomberg/BoF found one buyer owning 30 charms, the most expensive a $1,010 Louis Vuitton crab charm. Bloomingdale's Manhattan featured a $825 Prada robot charm alongside a $2,300 backpack. At this tier, buyers reason about material quality and brand coherence. Source: Business of Fashion / Bloomberg (Aug 2025).

- **Hermès / Chanel tier (€5,000+):** Buyers treat charms as brand-house collectibles or deliberate "playful" counterpoints to an investment bag.

**Implication for HBAG:** At €80–€150, the target buyer almost certainly owns a bag costing €600–€1,500 (LV/Gucci entry, Polene, Strathberry). The Coach/MK buyer will resist a €100 charm; the LV/Gucci-tier buyer finds it plausible if the material story is legible. **The addressable segment is luxury-adjacent and above, not accessible luxury.**

*Confidence: Inference from tier analysis — no published primary survey draws this exact line.*

### A2. Style Polarity: Which Is Driving the Trend Now

**Finding: Playful maximalism is the current driver, but craft/minimal is the emerging 2026 tailwind. Quiet luxury is the fading headwind.**

Hard data:
- Google Trends: "bag charms" searches up **168%** in 2025 vs. prior year. Source: JOOR Insights.
- Pinterest: +**700%** search growth May 2024–May 2025. Source: JOOR Insights.
- JOOR platform: **12× sales volume, 17× unit growth** in H1 2025 vs. H1 2024, **3× the number of brands** selling charms.

The growth is explicitly characterised as "the antidote to quiet luxury" — bag charms "play perfectly into fashion's recent maximalist direction." Valentino, Prada, YSL, Chloé, and Balmain all moved to bold aesthetics for 2025. Source: JOOR / Karve Meilleur 2025.

**2026 pivot (strategically important for HBAG):** WhoWhatWear 2026 trend analysis identifies a shift away from Labubu-style novelty toys toward:
1. **Texture and craft materiality** (Chloé Paddington with fur/leather stole keychains — natural materials).
2. **Repurposing and versatility** (charm-to-necklace dual function).

The 2026 direction is "vintage-inspired, no logos, sequins, embroidery, texture" — maps directly to artisan leather craft rather than Pop Mart plush. Source: WhoWhatWear Bag Charm Trends 2026.

**Implication for HBAG:** Entering at the right moment. The Labubu/toy phase is exhausting itself; craft/material authenticity is the next wave. **Position on materiality and craft, not character or novelty.**

### A3. Is It Luxury-Adjacent Buyers or True Luxury Owners?

**Finding: Primarily luxury-adjacent buyers treating charms as an accessible luxury; true luxury owners buying as a "play item" against an investment bag.**

- Inside Retail: "If handbags are investment pieces, charms are emotional purchases — their success hinging on the psychology of play and self-expression." Charm craze "dovetails with the broader dopamine dressing movement, where consumers seek joy through playful fashion — amid global economic uncertainty, small luxuries like charms provide an affordable form of escapism." Source: Inside Retail Asia, Aug 2025.
- Luxury brands introduced bag charms explicitly as entry-level items to "engage disenfranchised customers and blend luxury with affordability" while core handbag sales slumped. Source: JOOR.
- RealReal 2025 Resale Report: **340% spike** in bag charms, accessories, and brooches in resale — evidence of broad adoption across income tiers. Source: The RealReal 2025 Resale Report.

**The HBAG sweet spot:** Women spending €600–€1,500 on bags who would pay €80–€150 on a charm that "makes the bag feel hers." Predominantly Millennial/older Gen Z, 27–42, treat fashion as self-expression, deliberating 3–7 days online before purchasing.

## B. Purchase Triggers and Jobs-to-Be-Done

### B1. Purchase Triggers (Ranked)

1. **Trend exposure (TikTok/Instagram):** "Bag charm ideas" searches grew over **10,000%** from May 2024 to May 2025. Social media is the primary discovery mechanism. Coach credits viral social reach for its charm sales. Source: Glossy / Accio trend data.

2. **New bag acquisition (add-on trigger):** Coach and others train store associates to introduce charms at the point of bag sale — positioned as "the first thing you buy after getting a new bag." Source: Vogue College of Fashion — Coach personalisation strategy.

3. **Gifting:** Seasonal demand peaks correlate with Valentine's Day and holidays. "Personalised bag charms" reached peak normalised search interest of 99 in February 2026. Source: Accio.com trend data.

4. **Collectibility / repeat self-purchase:** Coach reports "seasonal charm drops now sell out within days" — habitual repeat-purchase from an engaged collector base. Source: BoF.

5. **Travel / destination (secondary):** Noted in editorial coverage as an occasion trigger, but secondary to the above.

*Confidence: Converging inference — no single consumer survey ranks triggers directly. Treat ordering as directional.*

---

### B2. Primary Job-to-Be-Done

**Finding: Personalisation + playful self-expression are co-equal primary jobs. Status signalling is secondary for this segment at the artisan tier.**

- Coach CEO language: "personalisation and customisation... a way to enhance self-expression." Source: Accio on Coach.
- Inside Retail: "charms are emotional purchases... the psychology of play." The buyer is giving herself permission to be playful with a serious luxury object.
- PurseForum qualitative: buyers discuss charms as making a widely-owned bag style feel uniquely "mine." The JTBD is solving the "it looks like everyone else's" problem without buying a new bag.

**For HBAG, the primary JTBD is:** "I want my bag to feel unmistakably mine — and I want the thing I put on it to look like it was made with real skill, not printed in China."

Status signalling is present but weaker than at the true-luxury (Hermès) level. Artisan/unbranded charms are purchased primarily for self-expression and craft appreciation. **Lean into: "unmistakably mine" + "real craft."**

---

### B3. Failure States (What Buyers Are Trying to Avoid)

**Ranked by expressed concern in forum and buyer data:**

1. **Looks/feels cheap or plasticky:** PurseForum users specifically flag "cheap plating scrubs off" even on designer charms; vintage charms "were made really well" — quality degradation is an active concern. "Sharp edges or uneven plating" are disqualifying signals. Sources: PurseForum discussion; Alibaba/Plantin buying guide.

2. **Damages the bag:** "Bag charms leave marks on bags" — a direct functional failure. A buyer who spent €800–€1,500 on a bag will not risk scratching it with poorly-finished attachment hardware. **The clip/attachment mechanism is a primary quality signal, not a secondary feature.** Source: PurseForum.

3. **Looks tacky or overdone:** PurseBlog ran "Unpopular Opinion: Don't Overdo Your Bag Charms." A segment worries about "trying too hard." This creates an opening for "one considered charm that elevates" vs. the maximalist pile-on. Source: PurseBlog opinion piece.

4. **Photographs poorly (inferred, not directly evidenced):** Given purchase discovery is TikTok/IG-driven and bags are social objects, a charm that doesn't photograph well is effectively dead in this market.

5. **Breaks quickly:** Referenced in quality guides and forum discussions about hardware and plating durability.

**Product implication:** Clip/attachment mechanism quality, finish consistency, and photography performance are not optional — they are the product's core risk surface.

## C. Price and Value Architecture

### C1. "Obvious Yes / Hesitate / No Way" Price Points

*No published Van Westendorp or conjoint analysis found specifically for bag charms or artisan leather accessories at €80–€150. This is a confirmed research gap. The following is triangulated from market comparables.*

**Market price bands observed:**
- Etsy/artisan DTC: leather bag charms and AirPod holders **$36–$120** (~€33–€110). Source: Etsy market listings.
- Coach tier (accessible luxury): $50–$95 for branded charms. $95 Cherry Bag Charm is a documented Gen Z bestseller. Source: Accio Coach data.
- Mid-luxury (Loewe, Miu Miu, Gucci): €150–€450 branded house charms.
- True luxury (LV, Prada, Chanel): $400–$1,200+.

**Triangulated price psychology for HBAG (artisan, unbranded at launch):**

| Zone | Price (EUR) | Interpretation |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Obvious yes | €79–€95 | Below €100 psychological barrier. Reads as "considered, not reckless." Impulse-adjacent for the LV-tier buyer. |

| No way (without brand proof) | €160+ | Requires exceptional photography, certifiable materials (e.g. full-grain Italian calf, named tannery), and social proof. High friction without brand equity. |

*Note: Coach's $95 charm (~€88) is the most relevant real-world anchor. An artisan with a stronger craft narrative can justify €10–€30 above this if material story is visible.*

**Recommended action:** Run a 50–100 person Van Westendorp survey via Typeform/Conjointly targeted at women who own bags in the €400–€1,500 range. Cost ~€200–€500. Would generate defensible price-banding data in 2–3 weeks. Source: Conjointly Van Westendorp tool.

### C2. Good / Better / Best Ladder Viability

**Finding: 2-tier approach recommended at launch; 3-tier is viable only after brand trust is established.**

Evidence for tiering:
- Handmade silk scarf analogue: $90–$120 (Classic) / $110–$140 (Premium small-run). A real-world artisan 2-step ladder.
- Leather goods pricing literature: Entry (Cost×2), Core (Cost×3), Premium (Cost×4+). At €25–€30 COGS: €50–€60 / €75–€90 / €100–€120. Broadly aligns with proposed ladder.

Evidence against 3 tiers at launch:
- Artisan DTC brands typically build equity faster with a single "hero SKU." The Coach Cherry Charm is a single-item viral phenomenon, not a three-tier offering.
- A €169 item with no brand halo faces very high conversion friction. Better positioned as a "collector edition" after reviews and social proof are established.

**Recommendation:** Launch with a clear anchor (€89–€99 core charm; €119 for AirPod holder version with additional functional utility). Introduce €149–€169 named-material tier (e.g. hand-burnished Buttero leather, brass hardware) after 10–20 reviews and social proof are in place.

## J. IP / Legal Risk — Birkin-Style Trade Dress

*Source: S0B deep research output, 2026-02-20. Specific court citation not independently verified — requires legal counsel confirmation before scaling.*

**Finding:** A 2025 decision of the Paris Judicial Court reinforces that iconic bag designs and related 3D trademarks can be protected and enforced in Europe — including against marketing of lookalike bags and even representations in NFTs. EU case law extends protection to signature closures and distinctive visual elements, not only to brand names or logos.

**Implication for HBAG:** The "Birkin-style" descriptor and any distinctive closure systems that closely replicate protected 3D marks create an EU-wide enforcement risk. This is a **base-asset constraint** — it applies to all product options, not to a single option, and should be treated as a pre-launch legal hygiene item.

**Required actions before scaling:**
1. Do not use the term "Birkin" in any public-facing marketing, product listings, or brand materials
2. De-risk distinctive closure/lock/strap visuals before EU/UK wholesale or destination retail launch
3. Obtain IP counsel review before committing to inventory at scale or entering institutional channels (hotels, boutiques) where partners are particularly risk-averse about trade dress
