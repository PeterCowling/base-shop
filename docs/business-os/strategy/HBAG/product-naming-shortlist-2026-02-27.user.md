## B) Curator's Analysis — Top Candidates by Territory

**T1 Form** (hardware and structure vocabulary): Fibbella (29/30) and Selletta (29/30) are the joint leads, both returning the maximum C=5 category signal — the hardware vocabulary (fibbia/clasp, sella/saddle) reads instantly as artisan accessories to the ICP without describing the product category in TM terms. Fibbella is the recommended selection for T1: "fibbia" is a more obscure root than "sella," giving higher TM distinctiveness, and the double-b creates a strong visual rhythm in the compound wordmark. Sellina (28/30) is the strongest secondary, with softer phonetics and slightly lower E headroom.

**T2 Bijoux** (jewellery and ornament vocabulary): Gemmella and Vezzella both score 29/30 — the strongest pair in the entire candidate set. Vezzella is the lead recommendation for T2: "vezzo" (charm/bead strand) directly echoes the bag-charm product use case without describing it, giving both TM ownability and instant category narrative. Ciondella and Pendella (both 28/30) are strong backups; Pendella reads slightly closer to "pendant" in English, which may attract attention in TM searches.

**T3 Craft** (artisan process vocabulary): Patinella (29/30) is the clear lead — the word "patina" is immediately understood in luxury fashion contexts (aged leather, surface quality) while being fully non-descriptive for TM purposes. Lucidella (28/30) is the strongest backup: lucidatura (polishing) is a more obscure root, trading ICP warmth for TM safety. Cesellova and Intarsella (both 27/30) carry the most distinctive craft stories (engraving, inlay) but are longer and require the operator to tell the story at point of sale.

**T4 Diminutive** (coined warm Italian forms): Lucella (28/30) is the lead: "luce" (light) is a widely understood quality word that feels aspirational without suggesting the product, and the 7-character wordmark is ideal for Etsy character constraints. Dolcella and Tenerella (both 27/30) are warm backups. Gaiella (27/30, I=5, C=2) has the highest distinctiveness but the lowest category signal — the operator would need to build the brand story around "joyful" without any accessor cues.

**T5 International** (French-root + Italian-diminutive hybrids): Nacrina and Dorella (both 29/30) are the co-leads. Nacrina is the recommended selection: "nacre" (mother-of-pearl) is embedded French fashion vocabulary that signals premium without being commonly used in accessories TM. Dorella ("gilded") is arguably more immediately readable but "oro/dore" territory has some existing Italian fashion TM coverage to verify. Aurella (28/30) is a safe backup with a classical Latin root that has no recent fashion conflicts.
