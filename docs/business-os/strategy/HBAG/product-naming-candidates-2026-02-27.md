## Summary

This round produced exactly 75 candidates distributed in equal fifths: 15 names per territory across T1 Form, T2 Bijoux, T3 Craft, T4 Diminutive, and T5 International. Score distribution is heavily skewed toward the high-priority band: 7 names scored 29 (the round ceiling, as no single name achieved the maximum 30 — every name had at least one dimension where the scored a 4 or lower due to genuine limitations in either category signal or ICP resonance), 55 names scored in the 24–28 range, and only 3 names fell into the secondary band at 22 (Bellovale, Borsina, Purina) — none scored 17 or below. In total, 62 of the 75 names qualify as high-priority (>=24) and are ready for TM pre-screen. The two strongest territories were T2 Bijoux and T5 International, which each placed two names in the top-7 scorers (Gemmella and Vezzella from T2; Nacrina and Dorella from T5): T2 names benefited from the dual lift of bijoux/charm vocabulary mapping onto the exact emotional register of the primary ICP (a woman who reads her handbag accessories as jewellery-adjacent) and onto category signal (pendant and charm vocabulary implies the product function without naming it as a "mini bag"), while T5 names — specifically the French-root + Italian-diminutive hybrids Nacrina and Dorella — achieved maximum distinctiveness because the coined blends exist in neither source language as dictionary entries, giving them strong TM ownability and a genuinely cross-border fashion register. T1 Form produced the joint-highest composite score in the table via Fibbella and Selletta (both 29), because hardware vocabulary (fibbia, sella) is both highly legible in Italian fashion context and non-descriptive enough for TM purposes, but other T1 names (Borsella, Borsina) were penalised on both D and E because the root borsa = bag creates a descriptiveness perception risk that hardware roots do not. T3 Craft scored strongly on D and C but more moderately on I across most names — craft vocabulary requires cultural context that the primary ICP responds to in an editorial setting but may not register instantly on a social-media scroll, explaining why Patinella and Lucidella (the T3 names with the most phonetically warm endings) outperformed harder craft terms like Conciella and Lucidova. T4 Diminutive achieved the highest I scores in the dataset — Lucella, Gaiella, Dolcella, and Tenerella all scored 5 on ICP resonance — because the warm coined-diminutive pattern mirrors the Caryina masterbrand's own morphological logic; the territory's main weakness is TM ownability risk for names derived from common Italian given names (Fiorella, Fiorina, Dolcina), which were penalised on D accordingly. Operators should prioritise the seven 29-point names — Fibbella, Gemmella, Nacrina, Dorella, Patinella, Selletta, Vezzella — for immediate TM landscape pre-screening, noting that Nacrina and Vezzella are the candidates most likely to combine strong TM ownability with expansion headroom beyond the hero mini bag.
